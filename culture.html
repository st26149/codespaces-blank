Sikhism is a religion that started in the Punjab region, which is now part of India and Pakistan. It was started by Guru Nanak Dev Ji (1469–1539), who taught that there’s only one God, everyone is equal, and we should try to live honestly and humbly. After him, nine other Gurus helped guide Sikhs and did some really remarkable things in the faith.

For example, Guru Angad Dev Ji made the Gurmukhi script we use today, Guru Amar Das Ji started the Langar (where everyone can eat together for free), Guru Ram Das Ji founded Amritsar and built the pool around the Golden Temple, and Guru Arjan Dev Ji finished the Golden Temple and wrote the first version of the Sikh holy book, the Adi Granth. The Golden Temple is open to everyone and shows that everyone is equal.

The tenth Guru, Guru Gobind Singh Ji (1666–1708), made the Sri Guru Granth Sahib Ji the eternal Guru for Sikhs and started the Khalsa, a group of Sikhs who focus on being brave, helping others, and staying disciplined (The Panj Pyaare). Sikhism teaches equality for everyone, doing selfless service (seva), meditating on God’s name (Naam Simran), and living honestly and kindly.

Today, millions of Sikhs follow these teachings and celebrate festivals like Vaisakhi and Gurpurabs. Sikhism is all about being fair, united, and devoted to both God and helping people.

Core Teachings

  • Equality for all
  • Seva (selfless service)
  • Naam Simran
  • Honesty and compassion
