Sikhism is a religion that began in the Punjab region of India and Pakistan. It was founded by Guru Nanak Dev Ji (1469–1539), who taught that there is only one God, all humans are equal, and we should live honestly and humbly.

After Guru Nanak Dev Ji, nine more Gurus continued to guide Sikhs. Guru Angad Dev Ji created the Gurmukhi script, Guru Amar Das Ji strengthened the tradition of Langar, Guru Ram Das Ji founded Amritsar, and Guru Arjan Dev Ji completed the Golden Temple and compiled the Adi Granth.

The tenth Guru, Guru Gobind Singh Ji, established the Khalsa and declared the Sri Guru Granth Sahib Ji as the eternal Guru. Sikhism teaches equality, selfless service (seva), meditation on God’s name (Naam Simran), and living truthfully.

Today, millions of Sikhs follow these teachings and celebrate festivals like Vaisakhi and Gurpurabs.

Core Teachings

  • Equality for all
  • Seva (selfless service)
  • Naam Simran
  • Honesty and compassion
